How NDIS Funding Works

NDIS funding refers to the money allocated in a participant’s NDIS plan to help access supports and services that are related to their disability and approved goals.

The amount of funding included in a participant’s plan is based on the participant’s individual circumstances, support needs, evidence provided, and the reasonable and necessary supports required to help them achieve their goals.

NDIS funding is designed to provide participants with access to the supports they need while considering their personal situation and the types of services that will best support their independence and wellbeing.

What NDIS Funding Can Be Used For

NDIS funding can be used to access approved NDIS supports and pay registered providers for services that align with the participant’s plan and disability-related needs.

Funding is organised into different support budgets, which help participants access supports across areas such as daily living, independence, personal care, and achieving their goals.

Depending on a participant’s individual needs and plan, funding may be available across different support categories. Every participant’s plan is different because support requirements vary from person to person.

NDIS funding may help cover supports such as:

  • Assistance with household tasks
  • Personal care and daily living support
  • Assistive technology, home modifications, or mobility equipment
  • Therapeutic and other approved supports

For more details, visit the NDIS website: What is NDIS funding?
